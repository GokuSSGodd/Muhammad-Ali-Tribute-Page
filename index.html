Muhammad Ali

The One Who Inspired Millions

Early Life

Muhammad Ali was born as Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. on January 17, 1942. Ali was born in Louisville Kentucky and was the oldest child. His father was a painter and his mother worked as a cleaning lady. When Ali turned 12 he began to box and devoted his life to boxing after Joe Martin, a police officer, brought him to a local youth center. Martin saw potential in Ali and Ali later started learning boxing from African American trainer Fred Stoner. Ali later went on to become one of the greatest boxers ever.

Greatest Accomplishments

  • 1990 International Boxing Hall of Fame
  • Won Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2005
  • Had 56 wins and 5 losses with 37 knockouts
  • First fighter to win the world heavyweight championship on 3 seperate occasions

Main Events in Ali's Life

  1. On March 6, 1964, Clay announced he was a Muslim and accepted the teachings of Islam, also formally changing his name to Muhammad Ali.
  2. On April 28, 1967, Ali refused induction into the U.S. Army and believed that he had no reason to fight agasint Vietnam.
  3. On June 20, 1967, Ali was criminally charged for refusing induction to the U.S. Army and was sentenced to 5 years in prison.
  4. 4 years later, the Supreme Court unanimously overturned Ali's conviction.
  5. On March 8, 1971 Ali challenged Joe Frazier for the first time and Frazier won an unanimous 15-round decision.
  6. The Second time Ali fought Frazier, Ali won an unanimous 12-round decision.
  7. Later on in Ali's life he indured damage to his brain from taking blows during his career and this resulted in slurred speech, slowed movement, and other symptons of Parkinson's disease.
  8. In the mid-1970s Ali began to study the Quran seriously and changed to Orthodox Islam.
  9. In 1986, Ali married his 4th wife Lonnie Ali and had 9 children together.
  10. In 1996, Ali was chosen to light the Olympic flame at the start of the XXVI Olympiad in Atlanta, Georgia.
  11. On June 3, 2016 Muhammad Ali passed away at the age of 74, missed by millions around the world.
